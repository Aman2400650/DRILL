Earthquake Preparedness

*Saftey Guidelines

1. Drop, Cover, And Hold On During Tremors.


2. Stay Away From Windows And Heavy Objects.


3. Keep An Emergency Kit Ready.


4. Move To An Open Space After The Shaking Stops.


*SUMMARY


1. Prepare Before an Earthquake

  • Check your house or building for structural safety.
  • Secure heavy items: shelves, cupboards, hanging objects, mirrors, TV, etc.
  • Keep an emergency kit ready: water, food, first-aid, flashlight, radio, batteries, important documents, medicines, and a power bank.
  • Make a family emergency plan: know safe spots in your home and where to meet if separated.


2. What to Do During an Earthquake

  • Stay away from windows, heavy furniture, and things that can fall.
  • If in a car, stop in an open, safe place and stay inside the vehicle.
  • If you are indoors — stay indoors; do NOT run outside during shaking.
  • If outside, move away from buildings, trees, power lines.


3. After the Earthquake

  • Check yourself and others for injuries; give first aid if possible.
  • Expect aftershocks — stay alert.
  • Check for gas leaks, electrical damage, water line breaks; turn off utilities if necessary.
  • Use phones only for emergencies to keep lines open.
  • Follow official instructions via radio, phone alerts, or government announcements.
